# Buchholz Relay and Power Transformer Protection

## 1. Importance of Power Transformers

* Power transformers are among the most important and expensive components in a power system.
* When properly maintained, they can operate for **30 years or more**.
* Transformer protection is essential because transformer failures can:

  * Interrupt power supply.
  * Cause production losses.
  * Lead to expensive repairs or replacement.
  * Affect the stability of the entire electrical network.

### Common Causes of Transformer Defects

* Expansion and contraction of windings.
* Overloading.
* Poor cooling.
* Insulation failure.
* Excessive heating.

---

## 2. Construction of a Power Transformer

A power transformer mainly consists of:

* **Primary winding**
* **Secondary winding**
* **Magnetic core**

In high-voltage transformers:

* The core and windings are enclosed inside a **main tank**.
* The tank protects internal components from:

  * Dust
  * Moisture
  * Physical damage

### Transformer Oil

The tank is filled with **transformer oil (mineral oil)**.

#### Functions of Transformer Oil

### 1. Insulation

* Has high dielectric strength.
* Prevents short circuits between transformer parts.

### 2. Cooling

* Absorbs heat generated by electrical losses.
* Transfers heat away from windings.
* Keeps the transformer within safe operating temperatures.

---

## 3. Conservator Tank

A **conservator tank** is mounted above the main transformer tank.

### Purpose

* Stores excess transformer oil.
* Compensates for oil expansion and contraction.
* Ensures sufficient oil remains in the main tank.

---

## 4. Buchholz Relay

### Definition

A **Buchholz Relay** is a gas-actuated protection device used only in **oil-filled transformers**.

### Location

Installed in the pipe connecting:

**Main Tank ⇄ Conservator Tank**

The relay is mounted at an inclination of approximately **2°–5°**.

---

## 5. Construction of Buchholz Relay

### Main Components

* Aluminum alloy housing
* Inspection windows
* Gas collection chamber
* Gas draw-off valve
* Upper float
* Lower float (or flap mechanism)
* Permanent magnets
* Magnetic reed contacts

### Magnetic Reed Contact

A reed contact is:

* A magnetically operated switch.
* Enclosed inside a sealed glass tube.
* Contains two ferromagnetic metal strips.
* Normally open under normal conditions.
* Closes when exposed to a magnetic field.

---

## 6. Gas Formation Inside Transformer

During operation:

* Windings generate heat.
* Excessive heat decomposes:

  * Transformer oil
  * Paper insulation

This decomposition produces gases such as:

* Hydrogen (H₂)
* Methane (CH₄)
* Acetylene (C₂H₂)

These gases rise upward and travel toward the conservator through the Buchholz relay.

---

## 7. Gas Collection and Analysis

The gas accumulates inside the Buchholz relay housing.

### Benefits

* Gas can be removed through the gas draw-off valve.
* Analysis of the gas helps identify:

  * Insulation deterioration
  * Internal transformer faults
  * Transformer health condition

---

# Operation of Buchholz Relay

## 8. Protection Against Minor Faults

Minor faults include:

* Overloading
* Core overheating
* Initial insulation breakdown

### Working

1. Fault generates gas slowly.
2. Gas accumulates in relay chamber.
3. Oil level inside relay decreases.
4. Upper float moves downward.
5. Magnet operates reed switch.
6. Alarm circuit is activated.

### Result

✅ Alarm signal only

This gives an early warning before serious damage occurs.

---

## 9. Protection Against Oil Leakage

### Working

1. Transformer oil leaks.
2. Oil level in conservator decreases.
3. Oil level inside Buchholz relay drops.

### Stage 1

* Upper float falls.
* Alarm switch operates.

**Result:** Alarm signal.

### Stage 2

If oil level continues to fall:

* Lower float also drops.
* Lower reed switch operates.

**Result:** Transformer trip signal.

The transformer is disconnected to prevent damage.

---

## 10. Protection Against Major Internal Faults

Major faults include:

* Insulation failure
* Inter-turn short circuit
* Internal short circuit

### Working

1. Serious fault generates a large volume of gas rapidly.
2. Gas rushes through the pipe toward the conservator.
3. High-speed oil movement strikes the relay flap.
4. Lower float/flap mechanism operates.
5. Reed contact closes.
6. Trip circuit is energized.

### Result

✅ Immediate transformer shutdown

This prevents catastrophic damage.

---

# Alarm and Trip Functions

| Condition                   | Relay Action              | Output |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------- | ------ |
| Small gas accumulation      | Upper Float Operates      | Alarm  |
| Oil leakage (initial stage) | Upper Float Operates      | Alarm  |
| Severe oil loss             | Lower Float Operates      | Trip   |
| Major internal fault        | Lower Float/Flap Operates | Trip   |

---

# Advantages of Buchholz Relay

* Detects faults at an early stage.
* Protects against internal transformer faults.
* Provides both alarm and trip functions.
* Helps prevent major transformer damage.
* Allows gas analysis for fault diagnosis.
* Simple and reliable operation.

---

# Quick Exam Definition

**Buchholz Relay:**
A gas-actuated protective relay installed between the main tank and conservator tank of an oil-filled transformer. It detects gas accumulation and oil movement caused by internal faults, providing alarm and trip signals to protect the transformer.
