# Three-Phase Electricity - Study Notes

## Introduction

Three-phase electricity is the most common method used for generating, transmitting, and distributing electrical power worldwide. It provides a good balance between efficiency, power delivery, and infrastructure cost.

To understand three-phase power, it is helpful to first understand how a simple AC generator works.

---

# 1. Simple AC Generator

A basic AC generator consists of:

* Copper wire coils placed inside a stator.
* A rotating magnet positioned between the coils.
* A complete electrical circuit connected to the coils.

When the magnet rotates, its magnetic field cuts across the coils and forces free electrons in the wire to move, producing an electric current.

---

# 2. How Alternating Current (AC) is Generated

As the magnet rotates:

1. The magnetic field strength increases through the coil.
2. Electrons accelerate in one direction.
3. At maximum magnetic field strength, current reaches its peak value.
4. As the magnetic field weakens, current decreases to zero.
5. The opposite magnetic pole then passes through the coil.
6. Electrons move in the opposite direction.
7. Current reaches a negative peak and returns to zero.

Because the current continuously changes direction, it is called **Alternating Current (AC)**.

---

# 3. AC Sine Wave

The output of an AC generator can be represented as a sine wave.

### Positive Half Cycle

* Current flows in one direction.
* Magnitude increases to a maximum value.
* Then decreases back to zero.

### Negative Half Cycle

* Current flows in the opposite direction.
* Magnitude increases to a negative maximum.
* Returns to zero.

One complete positive and negative cycle forms a **single AC cycle**.

---

# 4. Frequency

**Frequency** is the number of complete cycles produced every second.

### Unit

* Hertz (Hz)

### Common Frequencies

* 50 Hz (India, Europe, many other countries)
* 60 Hz (USA, Canada, and some other countries)

Example:

* 50 Hz = 50 complete AC cycles per second.
* Current changes direction 100 times per second.

---

# 5. Single-Phase Power

A single-phase generator contains one set of coils.

### Characteristics

* Produces one AC waveform.
* Power output rises and falls continuously.
* Voltage becomes zero twice during every cycle.

### Example

A lamp connected to a single-phase supply:

* Becomes brightest at voltage peaks.
* Becomes dimmer as voltage decreases.
* Briefly reaches zero output when voltage crosses zero.

Although household lights flicker, the frequency is so high that the human eye normally cannot detect it.

---

# 6. Two-Phase Power

A second set of coils can be added to the generator.

### Coil Placement

The second coil is positioned at a different angular location relative to the first coil.

### Advantage

* When one phase is decreasing, the other begins increasing.
* Reduces fluctuations in total power output.

### Limitation

Power gaps still exist, causing some variation.

---

# 7. Three-Phase Power

Adding a third coil creates a three-phase system.

### Coil Arrangement

The three coils are spaced equally around the stator:

* Phase A
* Phase B
* Phase C

Each phase is separated by **120 electrical degrees**.

### Result

* When one phase is decreasing, another is increasing.
* At least one phase is always near its peak value.
* Power delivery becomes much smoother and more constant.

---

# 8. Why Three-Phase Power is Preferred

### Advantages

#### Smooth Power Output

Power is delivered more continuously.

#### Higher Efficiency

More power can be transmitted using less conductor material.

#### Better Motor Performance

Three-phase motors:

* Start automatically.
* Produce smoother torque.
* Operate more efficiently.

#### Cost Effective

Provides excellent performance without requiring excessive wiring.

---

# 9. Why Not Four, Five, or More Phases?

Additional phases could make power even smoother.

However:

### Disadvantages

* More cables required.
* Higher installation cost.
* Increased maintenance complexity.

Three-phase power became the industry standard because it provides the best compromise between:

* Power quality
* System complexity
* Economic cost

---

# 10. Applications of Three-Phase Power

Three-phase systems are widely used for:

### Industrial Equipment

* Large motors
* Pumps
* Compressors
* Manufacturing machinery

### Commercial Buildings

* Elevators (lifts)
* HVAC systems
* Air-conditioning plants
* Large electrical loads

### Power Systems

* Power generation stations
* Transmission networks
* Distribution systems

---

# 11. Distribution of Three-Phase Power

Electricity is generally:

1. Generated as three-phase power.
2. Transmitted over long distances as three-phase power.
3. Distributed to consumers through transformers.

### Large Buildings

Three-phase power is supplied to the building.

Different loads are connected as needed:

#### Three-Phase Loads

* Lift motors
* Water pumps
* Air-conditioning systems

#### Single-Phase Loads

* Computers
* Office equipment
* Lighting circuits

---

# 12. Residential Supply

Most homes require relatively low power.

Therefore:

* Houses are usually connected to only one phase of the three-phase distribution system.
* Household appliances operate on single-phase power.

Large commercial or industrial facilities receive a full three-phase connection because their power requirements are much higher.

---

# Key Points Summary

* AC is generated by rotating a magnetic field through coils.
* Current continuously changes direction, creating a sine wave.
* Frequency is measured in Hertz (Hz).
* Single-phase power has significant power fluctuations.
* Three-phase power uses three waveforms separated by 120°.
* Three-phase systems provide smoother and more reliable power.
* Most electrical generation and transmission systems use three-phase power.
* Homes usually receive single-phase supply, while industries receive three-phase supply.
* Three-phase power is the optimal balance between performance and cost.
