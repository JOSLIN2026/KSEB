# Lightning Arrester (Metal Oxide Surge Arrester) – Complete Study Notes

## 1. Introduction

A **Lightning Arrester (Surge Arrester)** is a protective device used in power systems to protect equipment from:

* Lightning surges
* Switching surges
* Transient overvoltages

When lightning strikes a substation conductor, a huge amount of electrical energy enters the system within microseconds, causing the voltage to rise several times above its normal value.

Without protection, this can damage:

* Transformers
* Circuit breakers
* Bushings
* Insulators
* Other substation equipment

The lightning arrester safely diverts the surge current to earth and limits the voltage to a safe level.

---

# 2. Purpose of a Lightning Arrester

### Functions

* Protects equipment from overvoltage.
* Diverts surge current to earth.
* Prevents insulation breakdown.
* Improves reliability of the power system.

---

# 3. Types of Lightning Arresters

### Older Type

* Silicon Carbide (SiC) Gapped Arrester

### Modern Type

* Gapless Metal Oxide Arrester (MOA)

Advantages of MOA:

* Fast response
* High reliability
* Better protection
* No spark gaps required

---

# 4. Gapless Metal Oxide Arrester

### Meaning of Gapless

There is no air gap between:

* Live terminal
* Earth terminal

The active protection element is a column of:

**Metal Oxide Varistors (MOVs)**

---

# 5. Metal Oxide Varistor (MOV)

The MOV column is made by stacking many resistor discs.

### Main Materials

* Zinc Oxide (ZnO) – Primary material
* Bismuth Oxide
* Antimony Oxide

Additional dopants:

* Tin Oxide
* Cobalt Oxide
* Nickel Oxide

---

## Construction of MOV Stack

The varistor column consists of:

* Multiple MOV discs stacked together.
* Smooth electrode surfaces.
* Fiberglass support rods.
* Holding plates.
* Compression springs.

---

### Metal Oxide Varistor

* Not an ohmic material.
* Resistance changes with voltage.
* Very high resistance at normal voltage.
* Very low resistance during surges.

This property makes it ideal for lightning protection.

# 7. Nonlinear V-I Characteristic

### Under Normal Conditions

* Very high resistance.
* Only microampere leakage current flows.

### During Surge

* Resistance suddenly drops.
* Huge current flows to earth.
* Surge voltage is limited.

This is the fundamental operating principle of a metal oxide arrester.

# 11. Creepage Distance

### Definition

The shortest distance along the surface of an insulator between two conductive parts.

### Purpose

Prevents:

* Surface leakage current
* Flashover

---

## Sheds

The housing contains sheds (ribs).

Functions:

* Increase creepage distance.
* Improve insulation performance.
* Prevent surface tracking.

---

# 12. Pressure Relief System

Internal faults may create excessive pressure.

### Components

* Pressure relief diaphragm
* Aluminum flanges

Purpose:

* Release pressure safely.
* Prevent explosion of arrester housing.

---

# 13. Maximum Continuous Operating Voltage (MCOV)

### Definition

Maximum voltage at which an arrester can operate continuously.

### Requirement

MCOV should be at least:

**5% higher than the highest phase-to-earth voltage of the system.**

---

# 14. Leakage Current

During normal operation:

* Small current flows through the MOV column.
* Usually in microamperes.

This current is called:

### Leakage Current

Characteristics:

* Very small.
* Does not affect system operation.
* Does not create a fault.

---

# 15. Working of Lightning Arrester

## Normal Condition

### Step 1

System voltage ≤ MCOV

### Step 2

MOV offers very high resistance.

### Step 3

Only leakage current flows.

### Result

No effect on the system.

---

## Lightning Surge Condition

### Step 1

Lightning strikes transmission line or substation.

### Step 2

Voltage rises 3–10 times above normal.

### Step 3

MOV resistance suddenly decreases.

### Step 4

Large surge current flows through arrester.

### Step 5

Current is diverted safely to earth.

### Step 6

Voltage is limited to a safe value.

### Result

Equipment remains protected.

---

# 16. Current Path During Surge

Current flows:

**Live Terminal → MOV Stack → Earth Terminal → Ground**

---

# 17. Surge Counter

A surge counter is connected between:

**Earth Terminal and Ground**

### Functions

* Counts surge events.
* Monitors leakage current.
* Helps maintenance engineers assess arrester condition.

---

# 18. Energy Handling Capability

### Definition

Ability of the arrester to absorb surge energy without damage.

It is one of the most important specifications of a lightning arrester.

---

# 19. Thermal Stability

During operation:

* Leakage current produces heat.
* Heat must be dissipated to surroundings.

If heat generation exceeds heat dissipation:

* Temperature increases.
* Leakage current increases.
* More heat is generated.

This process may continue uncontrollably.

---

# 20. Thermal Runaway

### Definition

A condition in which rising temperature causes increasing leakage current, producing even more heat and eventually destroying the arrester.

### Sequence

1. Temperature rises.
2. Leakage current increases.
3. More heat is generated.
4. Temperature rises further.
5. Arrester fails.

---

# 21. Surge Current Capability

During severe lightning:

* Surge current may reach up to **200 kA**.
* Duration is only a few microseconds.

Even though the duration is short, a large amount of heat is produced inside MOV discs.

---
